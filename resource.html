Tips for Lost & Found Cats

If your cat is lost

  • Search nearby at dawn/dusk; check hiding spots (garages, sheds, under decks).
  • Post on neighborhood apps/groups (Nextdoor, local Facebook groups).
  • Notify shelters, Animal Control, and nearby vets (give a recent photo).
  • Leave food, water, and a familiar scent (blanket/used litter) near home.
  • Update our Lost Cats list with details and contact info.

If you found a cat

  • Check for ID tag; ask neighbors. Many vets/shelters scan microchips for free.
  • Take a clear photo and post flyers + neighborhood apps.
  • Add the cat to our Found Cats list or report it here.
  • Keep the cat safe and separated from your pets until identified.

Microchipping & ID

Microchips greatly increase the chance of a reunion. Ask a vet or shelter to scan for one. Make sure your microchip is registered and your contact info is up-to-date.

  • Get your cat microchipped at a vet/shelter (often low-cost events are available).
  • Register and keep your phone/email current with the microchip registry.
  • Use a breakaway collar with a readable ID tag as a visible backup.

Preventive Care & Safety

  • Keep cats indoors or supervise outdoor time; consider a secure “catio.”
  • Spay/neuter to reduce roaming and help community cats.
  • Stay current on vaccinations and wellness checks.
  • Cat-proof balconies/windows; use screens and secure plants (avoid toxic ones).
